My trajectory

Born in São Luís in the state of Maranhão, Brazil, since I was a child I had the dream and aspiration of becoming an academic. Most likely motivated by the figure of my parents, professionals and researchers who are excellent in their fields. At the age of 17 I enrolled in the Bachelor's degree in Nutrition at the Federal University of Maranhão. There I took full advantage of everything the university had to offer me and I also matured. I was a course monitor, participated in conferences and events, coordinated research and extension projects and, in the end, the flame that had burned since childhood to become an academic only grew stronger.

I graduated in 2017 and in the following years I improved my skills and knowledge. My former teachers, now colleagues, became collaborators in my studies, ideas and projects. In 2018, alongside my former professor and current PhD advisor, I participated in my first project funded by a national research institution. I improved my skills in the field of biostatistics, specifically in conducting systematic reviews and meta-analyses. I traveled to the Federal University of Pelotas in Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil to learn how to conduct a meta-analysis and returned to Maranhão to share my knowledge with my peers. Subsequently, I gradually contributed more to the field of nutrition, with an emphasis on epidemiology and public health.

In 2021, I began my master's degree studies supervised by a reference in statistics and biostatistics in Brazil. I gradually moved towards the application of statistical methods and models in different topics of epidemiology and public health. I fell in love with food consumption analysis and wrote my master's dissertation on the construction of different food consumption scales using Item Response Theory.

As hasty and precocious as I have always been, I had barely finished my master's degree and was already accepted for my doctorate. Driven by my curiosity to better understand the nuances of food consumption, how it occurs in different scenarios, whether on a personal or environmental level, I delved deep into public data sets on food consumption in Brazil and unraveled its aspects, motivations and limitations from different perspectives (always applying robust statistical models, something that had already become ingrained in my genetic code).

Motivated by my advisor, I went to my first scientific event outside my area of ​​nutrition or epidemiology. I discussed models, learned about what was being proposed at the forefront of statistics and met important people who would become constant presences in my life and emissaries of opportunities and good news. I left the event with the intention of further improving my knowledge and venturing into new and promising areas to better understand my outcomes.

That conference was a turning point in my life, since I had the opportunity to meet my current supervisor, someone with so much knowledge, eager to share her knowledge and so much humility. In November 2023 we talked and agreed to try to do a research internship together at her institution, McGill University. In November 2024, I traveled to Montreal to begin said studies with her.

What will happen in the future? I will continue researching, deepening my skills and sharing the knowledge I gain with my peers. After all, science is a joint effort.
